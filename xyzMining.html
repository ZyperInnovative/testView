XYZ Quantum Miner FAQ

×
What is XYZ Quantum Miner?

XYZ Quantum Miner is a browser-based mining engine that utilizes GPU acceleration and network bandwidth to perform distributed cryptographic computations and mine XYZ tokens while your device remains online.

How does the mining process work?

The miner runs lightweight tasks in the background, using your GPU and occasional internet bandwidth to contribute to XYZ's distributed computing network. Rewards are calculated at a fixed rate of 0.007 XYZ per 24 hours of active participation.

Why do I need SOL balance?

A small amount of SOL (0.00005 SOL) is needed to pay for Solana network gas fees when transferring your mined XYZ tokens to your wallet. Without this, token payouts cannot be processed.

What happens when I'm offline?

Mining pauses automatically when you go offline. Your current progress and pending balance are saved in real time, and will resume once you're back online.

When will I receive my mined XYZ?

Payouts are processed daily at 11:59 PM PST, provided you have mined XYZ tokens and enough SOL in your wallet to cover the transaction fee.

Does this use my device's resources?

Yes. XYZ Quantum Miner utilizes a small portion of your GPU for computation and limited bandwidth to participate in the decentralized network. It's optimized to minimize impact on performance while running in the background.
